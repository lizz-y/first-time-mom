Feeding Your Newborn

Whether you're breastfeeding, formula-feeding, or doing a bit of both — newborns eat often! Expect 8-12 feedings a day. Watch for hunger cues like lip-smacking and rooting.

Newborn Sleep Basics

Newborns sleep a lot — often 16-18 hours a day — but in short stretches. It's totally normal for sleep to feel unpredictable the first few weeks. Practice safe sleep: always place baby on their back in a crib or bassinet.

  • Safe to Sleep (NIH)
  • Newborn sleep tips

Diapering & Hygiene

Expect 6+ wet diapers a day and several poopy ones too! Newborns need frequent diaper changes and gentle skin care. Use fragrance-free wipes and apply diaper cream as needed.

  • How to diaper a baby (KidsHealth)
  • Preventing diaper rash

Baby's Health & Checkups

Your baby will have their first pediatrician visit within a few days of birth. Keep an eye on temperature, signs of jaundice, or anything that feels “off.” Trust your instincts — if you’re concerned, call your doctor.
